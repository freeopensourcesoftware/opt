NVIDIA GPU Tweaks
├── Search "NVIDIA Control Panel"
├── Under "3D Settings"
│   └── Go into "Manage 3D settings"
│       └── Under "Global Settings"
│           ├── Change:
│           │   ├── "Anisotropic filtering" to "Application-controlled"
│           │   ├── "Antialiasing - Mode" to "Application-controlled"
│           │   ├── "Antialiasing - Setting" to "Application-controlled"
│           │   ├── "CUDA - Sysmem Fallback Policy" to "Default"
│           │   ├── "OpenGL GDI compatibility" to "Auto"
│           │   ├── "OpenGL rendering GPU" to "your gpu"
│           │   ├── "Power management mode" to "Prefer maximum performance"
│           │   ├── "Preferred refresh rate" to "Highest available"
│           │   ├── "Shader Cache Size" to "10GB"
│           │   ├── "Texture filtering - Negative LOD bias" to "Allow"
│           │   └── "Texture filtering - Quality" to "High performance"
│           └── Off:
│               ├── "Image Scaling"
│               ├── "Ambient Occlusion"
│               ├── "Antialiasing - FXAA"
│               ├── "Antialiasing - Transparency"
│               ├── "Background Application Max Frame Rate"
│               ├── "DSR - Factors"
│               ├── "DSR - Smoothness"
│               ├── "Low Latency Mode"
│               ├── "Max Frame Rate"
│               ├── "Multi-Frame Sampled AA (MFAA)"
│               ├── "Texture filtering - Trilinear optimization"
│               ├── "Triple buffering"
│               ├── "Vertical sync"
